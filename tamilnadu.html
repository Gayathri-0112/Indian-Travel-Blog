Must Visit @ TAMIL NADU

Tamil Nadu is often referred to as the "Land of Tamils" or the "Tamizhagam" due to its historical connection with the Tamil-speaking people. Additionally, it is sometimes called the "Land of Temples" because of the numerous ancient and intricately designed temples scattered across the state. Its capital city is CHENNAI.

(1)Chennai: The capital city known for Marina Beach, Kapaleeshwarar Temple, and Fort St. George.

(2)Mahabalipuram (Mamallapuram): Famous for UNESCO World Heritage Sites like Shore Temple, Arjuna's Penance, and the Five Rathas.

(3)Kanchipuram: Known for its temples, including Kailasanathar Temple, Ekambareswarar Temple, and the city's silk industry.

(4)Madurai: Home to the Meenakshi Amman Temple, Thirumalai Nayakkar Palace, and the bustling Meenakshi Bazaar.

(5)Ooty (Udhagamandalam): A popular hill station with attractions like Ooty Lake, Botanical Gardens, and the Nilgiri Mountain Railway.

(6)Kodaikanal: Another picturesque hill station with attractions like Kodai Lake, Coaker's Walk, and Bryant Park.

(7)Rameswaram: A sacred pilgrimage site known for Ramanathaswamy Temple, Agni Theertham, and Pamban Bridge.

(8)Kanyakumari: The southernmost tip of India, known for Vivekananda Rock Memorial, Thiruvalluvar Statue, and the meeting point of three seas.

(9)Thanjavur: Famous for Brihadeeswarar Temple, Thanjavur Palace, and Saraswathi Mahal Library.

(10)Kumbakonam & Dhanushkodi: Known for its temples, including Adi Kumbeswarar Temple, Sarangapani Temple, and the Mahamaham Tank. Dhanushkodi is a ghost town near Rameswaram, known for its ruins and the Ram Setu (Adam's Bridge).
