Must Visit @ ANDHRA PRADESH

Andhra Pradesh is often referred to as the "Rice Bowl of India" due to its significant contribution to the country's rice production. Its capital city is AMARAVATI.

(1)Tirupati: Renowned for the Sri Venkateswara Temple, one of the richest and most visited religious sites globally.

(2)Visakhapatnam (Vizag): A coastal city with picturesque beaches like RK Beach and Ramakrishna Beach. Submarine Museum, Kailasagiri Hill, and Borra Caves are popular attractions.

(3)Amaravati: The new capital city of Andhra Pradesh, known for the Amaravati Stupa and the Amareswara Temple.

(4)Vijayawada: Prakasam Barrage, Kanaka Durga Temple, and Bhavani Island are notable attractions.

(5)Warangal: The Thousand Pillar Temple, Bhadrakali Temple, and the Warangal Fort showcase the region's historical and architectural significance.

(6)Guntur: Known for Amaravathi, an ancient Buddhist site with stupas and sculptures. Kondaveedu Fort and Undavalli Caves are worth exploring.

(7)Kurnool: Oravakallu Rock Garden, Belum Caves (largest cave system in the Indian subcontinent), and the Kurnool Fort are key attractions.

(8)Nellore: Famous for its historic temples like Sri Ranganatha Temple and Udayagiri Fort.

(9)Rajahmundry: Papi Hills, Godavari Bridge, and Dowleswaram Barrage are prominent places to visit.

(10)Srikakulam: Arasavalli Sun Temple and Kalingapatnam are cultural and historical sites in this northern district.
