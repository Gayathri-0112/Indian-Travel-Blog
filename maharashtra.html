Must Visit @ MAHARASHTRA

Maharashtra is often referred to as the "Land of Marathas" due to its historical association with the Maratha Empire. Additionally, it is sometimes called the "Gateway to the Heart of India" because of its central location and significant economic and cultural contributions to the country. Its capital city is MUMBAI.

(1)Mumbai: The bustling financial capital, known for the Gateway of India, Marine Drive, and iconic landmarks like the Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus.

(2)Pune: A vibrant city with historical sites like Shaniwar Wada, Aga Khan Palace, and the Osho International Meditation Resort.

(3)Aurangabad: Home to the UNESCO World Heritage Sites of Ajanta and Ellora Caves, showcasing ancient rock-cut architecture and art.

(4)Nashik: Famous for the Kumbh Mela, Trimbakeshwar Temple, and vineyards producing renowned Indian wines.

(5)Mahabaleshwar: A popular hill station known for its scenic landscapes, strawberry farms, and the Venna Lake.

(6)Lonavala and Khandala: Picturesque hill stations with lush greenery, waterfalls, and viewpoints like Tiger's Point and Rajmachi Fort.

(7)Elephanta Island (Gharapuri): Home to the Elephanta Caves, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, featuring ancient rock-cut sculptures and temples.

(8)Shirdi: A significant pilgrimage site known for the Sai Baba Temple and the peaceful Shirdi Sai Baba Samadhi Mandir.

(9)Ajanta and Ellora Caves: Ancient rock-cut cave complexes near Aurangabad, showcasing exquisite Buddhist, Jain, and Hindu sculptures.

(10)Bhandardara: A serene hill station with attractions like the Arthur Lake, Randha Falls, and the Ratangad Fort.
