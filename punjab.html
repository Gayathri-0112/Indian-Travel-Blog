Must Visit @ PUNJAB

Punjab is often referred to as the "Land of Five Rivers" due to the five major rivers that flow through the region. Additionally, it is sometimes called the "Granary of India" because of its fertile agricultural lands. Its capital city is CHANDIGARH.

(1)Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib), Amritsar: The holiest shrine in Sikhism, known for its golden architecture, serene sarovar, and spiritual ambiance.

(2)Wagah Border, Amritsar: Famous for the daily ceremonial closing of the India-Pakistan border gates, known as the Wagah Border Ceremony.

(3)Jallianwala Bagh, Amritsar: A memorial garden commemorating the Jallianwala Bagh massacre, a significant event in Indian history.

(4)Sheesh Mahal, Patiala: Part of the Qila Mubarak complex, known for its exquisite mirror work and architecture.

(5)Ranjit Singh Museum, Amritsar: Dedicated to Maharaja Ranjit Singh, showcasing artifacts, weapons, and paintings from the Sikh Empire.

(6)Qila Mubarak, Patiala: A historical fort with beautiful architecture, including the Sheesh Mahal and the Darbar Hall.

(7)Fateh Burj, Mohali: The tallest victory tower in India, commemorating the victory of the Sikhs in the Battle of Chappar Chiri.

(8)Maharaja Ranjit Singh War Museum, Ludhiana: A museum dedicated to the life and achievements of Maharaja Ranjit Singh and the Indian armed forces.

(9)Gobindgarh Fort, Amritsar: A historic fort with museums, exhibitions, and a sound and light show.

(10)Durgiana Temple, Amritsar: A Hindu temple with architectural similarities to the Golden Temple, dedicated to Goddess Durga.
