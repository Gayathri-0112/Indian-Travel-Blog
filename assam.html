Must Visit @ ASSAM

Assam is often referred to as the "Gateway to the Northeast" due to its strategic location as the primary entry point to the northeastern region. Additionally, it is sometimes called the "Land of Red Rivers and Blue Hills" because of its numerous rivers and the scenic hills of the eastern Himalayas. Its capital city is DISPUR.

(1)Kaziranga National Park: Home to the one-horned Indian rhinoceros, Kaziranga is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a significant wildlife sanctuary.

(2)Guwahati: The largest city in Assam, featuring the Kamakhya Temple, Umananda Island, and the vibrant Brahmaputra River.

(3)Manas National Park: Another UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its rich biodiversity and the Manas River flowing through the park.

(4)Majuli Island: The world's largest river island on the Brahmaputra, known for its unique culture, Vaishnavite monasteries (Satras), and scenic landscapes.

(5)Sivasagar: Former capital of the Ahom Kingdom, showcasing historical monuments like the Rang Ghar, Talatal Ghar, and the Sivasagar Tank.

(6)Jorhat: Known for its tea gardens, Jorhat is a cultural hub with attractions like the Majuli River Island, Thengal Manor, and Cinnamara Tea Estate.

(7)Tezpur: Offers historical sites like Agnigarh, Mahabhairab Temple, and a scenic view of the Brahmaputra River.

(8)Hajo: A pilgrimage site with diverse religious significance, housing the Hayagriva Madhava Temple, Powa Mecca, and the Hajo Powa Mecca.

(9)Dibrugarh: A major tea-producing region with lush green tea estates, offering opportunities for river cruises on the Brahmaputra.

(10)Kamakhya Temple: Located in Guwahati, this famous Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Kamakhya is a major pilgrimage site.
