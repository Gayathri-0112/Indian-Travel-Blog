Must Visit @ WEST BENGAL

West Bengal is often referred to as the "Cultural Capital of India" due to its rich cultural heritage, literature, arts, and intellectual pursuits. Additionally, it is sometimes called the "City of Joy" due to the lively and vibrant atmosphere of its capital, Kolkata. Its capital city is KOLKATA.

(1)Kolkata: The cultural capital known for Victoria Memorial, Howrah Bridge, Indian Museum, and Dakhineshwar Kali Temple.

(2)Darjeeling: A popular hill station known for the Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, Tiger Hill, and tea plantations.

(3)Sundarbans: The largest mangrove forest, known for its unique ecosystem, Royal Bengal tigers, and boat safaris.

(4)Siliguri: Gateway to the Northeast, known for Mahananda Wildlife Sanctuary, Kali Mandir, and the ISKCON Temple.

(5)Kalimpong: A serene hill station known for Deolo Hill, Zang Dhok Palri Phodang, and the Pine View Nursery.

(6)Dakshineswar Kali Temple: A famous Kali temple on the banks of the Hooghly River, known for its association with Sri Ramakrishna Paramahamsa.

(7)Bishnupur: Known for terracotta temples, including the Rasmancha, Jorbangla Temple, and the Madan Mohan Temple.

(8)Jaldapara Wildlife Sanctuary: Known for its population of Indian one-horned rhinoceros, elephants, and diverse wildlife.

(9)Bankura: Known for Susunia Hill, Biharinath Hill, and the ancient Bishnupur terracotta temples.

(10)Malda: Historical city with attractions like the Adina Mosque, Gour, and the Firoz Minar.
